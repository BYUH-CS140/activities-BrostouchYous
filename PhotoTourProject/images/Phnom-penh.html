PHNOM PENH

Phnom Penh is the capital and largest city of Cambodia located at the confluence of the Mekong River and Tonlé Sap River. It is the political, economic, and cultural center of the country. The city is famous for its French colonial architecture, its busy marketplaces, and its historical monuments such as the Royal Palace, the Silver Pagoda, and the National Museum.

In addition, Phnom Penh reflects Cambodia’s modern growth alongside its complicated and sometimes painful past. The Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum and the Killing Fields of Choeung Ek are chilling reminders of the Khmer Rouge era. Nevertheless, Phnom Penh is a thriving city with a burgeoning skyline, delicious street food, and friendly local culture.
