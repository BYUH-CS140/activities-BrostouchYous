As cities grow and forests are cut down, many Romdoul trees are being lost. To protect this special flower, Cambodian people are planting it in schools, parks, gardens, and pagodas. Some groups also teach others about its meaning and how to take care of it. By saving the Romdoul, Cambodians keep a part of their identity and natural beauty alive. Romdoul is the national flower of Cambodia.

Romdoul symbolizes beauty, elegance, and the rich natural heritage of Cambodia. It often appears in poems, songs, and traditonal stories. Many Cambodians plant it in their gardens. This flower reminds people of Cambodian identity and pride. It is also used in festivals and cultural ceremonies. Cambodian children are sometimes named after the flower because of its loving meaning.

This flower is known for its sweet fragrance and creamy yellow-white petals. It blooms in the evening and is cherished in Cambodian culture. This flower grows on a small tree and is often planted near homes. Its scent is strongest at night and is loved by many Cambodians.
